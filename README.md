# 🚀 MCT India Summit Frontier Hack Event

Welcome to the **MCT India Summit Frontier Hack Event**! This hack is designed **by MCTs, for MCTs**—focusing on the real challenges Microsoft Certified Trainers face **before**, **during**, and **after** training delivery.

This is not about building a perfect product. It is about **problem framing, collaboration, and ideation** using Copilot and AI as thinking partners.

---

## 🎯 Goal

Accelerate alignment on the *Frontier Firm* mindset by:
* Identifying high-impact challenges faced by **Microsoft Certified Trainers**.
* Exploring how **AI and Copilot** can reduce friction in training delivery.
* Producing clear, actionable solution ideas that can scale across the MCT community.

The emphasis is on **thinking clearly, collaborating effectively, and experimenting boldly**.

---

## 🔄 Event Flow (70 Minutes Active Hack)

* **0:00 - 0:10 | Kickoff & Team Formation:** Meet your table, assign roles, and choose a track.
* **0:10 - 0:25 | Define the Challenge:** Identify a specific MCT pain point and draft a problem statement.
* **0:25 - 0:55 | Explore AI Solutions:** Ideate and build with Copilot, Researcher, or Copilot Studio.
* **0:55 - 1:10 | Make the Case & Submit:** Finalize your Word template and submit your idea.

---

## 🛠️ Step 1: Kickoff & Team Formation (10 Minutes)

The clock is ticking. Your first goal is to organize, align, and assign roles so you can start building immediately.

Download this word template ([MCT India Hack template](https://1drv.ms/w/c/0b2f613037d0a8c5/IQCW2gzrzo6eTYYkcF4-fr8LAbSOWCHRxYqMFJO0bZ_VKMw?e=CwZkMZ)) that will be required at the end for final submission

* **Find Your Crew:** Introduce yourself to your table and quickly establish the technical background of your team members.
* **Meet Your Table Coach:** Your coach is your guide and tie-breaker. If the team is stuck deliberating on an idea for more than 3 minutes, the coach will force a decision.
* **Assign Roles:** Divide and conquer. Assign a **Scribe** (to manage the Word Template), a **Builder** (to drive Copilot/Agents), and a **Presenter** (to refine the core problem statement).
* **Pick a Track Fast:** Vote immediately on tackling a **Pre-Delivery**, **In-Delivery**, or **Post-Delivery** challenge.

---

## 🕵️‍♂️ Step 2: Define the Challenge (15 Minutes)

Do not jump to the solution yet. A well-defined problem is half the battle. Use this time to narrow your focus to a single, solvable pain point.

* **Pre-Delivery Ideas:** Courseware ramp-up time, content overload, or customizing standard labs/demos for specific client industries.
* **In-Delivery Ideas:** Learner attention fatigue, managing mixed-skill audiences, or strict time management during live training.
* **Post-Delivery Ideas:** Analyzing student feedback (MTMs), creating personalized follow-up learning paths, or versioning reusable class assets.
* **Draft the Problem Statement:** Scribes, capture this in your template: *"As an MCT, the most frustrating part of [Track] is [Specific Task] because [Root Cause]. This results in [Impact]."*
* **Validate with Copilot:** Use Copilot (Work mode) as a thinking partner to break the problem down into smaller, solvable pieces.

---

## 💡 Step 3: Explore AI Solutions & Build (30 Minutes)

This is the core execution phase. Balance AI suggestions with team discussion—neither replaces the other.

* **Ideate:** Ask Copilot how it would solve the specific problem statement you just drafted.
* **Prototype:** Start building the solution. This could be a finely tuned prompt flow, an automated workflow, or a basic Copilot Studio agent.
* **Capture Insights:** Document what works and what fails. If an AI approach hits a wall, pivot quickly and note the limitation in your template.
* **Consult Your Coach:** If you hit a technical blocker, pull in your Table Coach to help unblock the team and keep the momentum going.

---

## 📝 Step 4: Make the Case & Submit (15 Minutes)

Stop building and start summarizing. A great idea only wins if it is clearly communicated.

* **Finalize the Template:** The **MCT Hack Event Template (Word)** is your final required submission. Ensure all sections are complete and concise.
* **Link the Artifact:** Include the exact prompt text, the logic flow, or a link to your deployed agent in your final summary.
* **Submit:** Follow the instructions provided by the organizers to upload or hand in your completed Word document.

---

## 🏆 Evaluation Criteria

Final submissions are required to qualify and will be assessed on:

* **Strategic Alignment:** Does this matter to MCTs?
* **Quantifiable Value:** Time saved, effort reduced, quality improved.
* **Community Impact:** Applicability across the broader MCT community.
* **Sustainability & Scalability:** Can this solution be maintained and shared?
* **Clarity of Outcome:** Is the solution clearly explained in the template?

*AI will score first; judges will review.*
